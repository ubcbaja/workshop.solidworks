Introduction

What is Bottum-up design?
  • Bottom-up design is the traditional method
  • You first design and model parts, then insert them into an assembly
What is Top-down design?
  • Top-down design is also referred to as in-context design
  • In Top-down design, parts' shapes, sizes, and locations can be designed in the assembly

Source: https://help.solidworks.com/2020/English/SolidWorks/sldworks/c_Design_Methods.htm?id=8f88a6e39efd495bb8877c159b76a33f#Pg0

General Suggestions

NOTE: The following suggestions are unrelated to today's exercise.

I've noticed some specific areas where students consistently encounter difficulties and hope that the following suggestions can help improve your experience.

Avoid saving parts with "Rolled Back" States. This tool is mostly for making temporary changes to your part or assembly. Otherwise, you are constantly prompted when opening assemblies to roll the part forward and often the "forgotten" features that have been rolled back will cause mating errors if rolled forward - The end user usually doesn't know if the part should be rolled forward or left as is. If you need multiple configurations, use Configurations (Refer to SolidWorks Help).
